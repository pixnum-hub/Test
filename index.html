Interactive route map

South Col & North Ridge, side by side

An illustrated elevation diagram of Everest's two principal climbing routes. Waypoint markers show major camps and landmarks; smaller markers indicate the approximate, publicly documented location associated with a climber record. All positions are stylized and approximate — this is not a survey map or a source of precise coordinates.

Drag not required — tap or click any marker

Elevation positions are illustrative and vertically compressed for readability; horizontal placement separates the South Col (left/lower approach) and North Ridge (right/lower approach) routes for comparison. Locations for individual climber records are approximate, drawn from public reporting, and are not derived from GPS tracking data.

About this project

Purpose, sources & preferences

Why this archive exists

This project documents the human history of Mount Everest — its routes, its hazards, and the people who have died attempting to climb it — for educational purposes. It is built entirely from publicly available reporting, expedition records, and published mountaineering histories. It does not use, request, or display private or undisclosed location data.

A note on respect

Every record here represents a real person and a grieving family. Locations are described only in general, publicly reported terms. Where individual details are not part of the public record, this archive says so plainly rather than speculating.

Sources

Records draw on outlets and archives including the Himalayan Database, the American Alpine Club, the Royal Geographical Society, BBC News, National Geographic, and published first-person mountaineering accounts. Each record lists its specific sources.
