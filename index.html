✦ Four Sacred Systems ✦

𝟗

Pythagorean Numerology

The most widely used Western system, attributed to the Greek mathematician Pythagoras (569–470 BCE). Numbers 1–9 are assigned sequentially to the alphabet A=1 through I=9, then repeating. It emphasises the rational, psychological dimensions of the self and is the foundation of modern Western numerology.

𒀭

Chaldean Numerology

One of the oldest known numerological systems, originating in ancient Babylon (Chaldea). It assigns values 1–8 to letters — the number 9 is considered sacred and is never directly assigned. Chaldean numerology is considered more spiritually accurate by many practitioners as it is based on the vibrational frequency of each letter rather than alphabetical sequence.

Vedic Numerology (Sankhya)

Rooted in the ancient Indian science of Jyotish (Vedic astrology), Vedic numerology links each number 1–9 to a planet: 1–Sun, 2–Moon, 3–Jupiter, 4–Rahu, 5–Mercury, 6–Venus, 7–Ketu, 8–Saturn, 9–Mars. It focuses on three core numbers: the Psychic Number (day of birth), Destiny Number (full DOB), and Name Number. Each carries planetary energy and karmic significance.

Kabbalah Numerology

Emerging from the mystical Jewish tradition of Kabbalah, this system analyses only the name — not the birth date. The Hebrew alphabet has 22 letters, each with a numerical value. When applied to English names, Kabbalah numerology uses values 1–9 assigned to the 26 English letters in a specific Hebrew-influenced pattern. It focuses on a single Name Number that reveals one's soul vibration and spiritual mission.
